# Academic Notes: Kinship Graph Typologies & Threat Dynamics

## 1. Non-Monotonicity and Percolation Thresholds
Our empirical tuning data reveals that genetic vulnerability does not scale linearly with cousin connectivity. 
As seen in our calibration curves, network density reaches a localized phase transition. 
Beyond `cp = 0.20`, supplementary biological edges introduce network cycles (redundancies) rather than path extensions to unexposed clusters. 
This implies that security defenses on DTC platforms are highly sensitive to regional kinship structures up to a specific density threshold, after which exposure risk flattens before spiking again.

## 2. The 3-Hop Security Perimeter
Because exposure completely saturates at 3 hops, mitigation and notification frameworks do not need to calculate infinite kinship cascades. 
A 3-hop radius (bounding up to second/third-degree relatives) represents the complete mathematical perimeter of a threat actor's reach.

## 3. Asymmetric Vulnerability in Emerging Databases
The high risk score of the Research Biobank (6.34) is a critical finding. 
It indicates that even if an institution does not market a "DNA Relatives" matching feature, the passive correlation of family members participating in the same study constructs an implicit kinship graph capable of an 80x amplification footprint during a database breach.
